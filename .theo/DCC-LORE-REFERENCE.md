This document serves as a comprehensive GM reference guide for running a Dungeon Crawler Carl (DCC) TTRPG homebrew campaign. It outlines core dungeon mechanics, progression systems, key NPCs, and session-specific details.

## The Dungeon System
- **18-Floor Gauntlet**: The dungeon consists of 18 floors. Floor 1 is the tutorial, featuring a massive grid of 30'x30' tunnels.
- **Floor Mechanics**: Each floor has a unique theme, layout, and a collapse mechanic. When crawlers descend, the previous floor collapses, killing anyone left behind.
- **The System AI**: A Macro AI grafted into the planet's Primal Engine runs everything. It is petulant, snarky, and values entertainment above all else. Refers to itself as "Daddy" when talking to Carl. It has discretionary control over achievements and loot boxes up to the Platinum tier.
- **Protections Mechanic**: Admins, Tourists, and Managers have a sliver of health reserved; they are teleported away before taking a fatal blow. However, due to corporate cost-cutting, these protections do not activate until AFTER Floor 3.

## Game Guides
- **Role**: Non-combatant NPCs who act as guildmasters of Tutorial Guild Halls on Floors 1-3.
- **Background**: Almost all are former crawlers who accepted Exit Deals. They typically serve 100 cycles (16-34 standard years each), with the time adjusted based on their crawler's progression. Guides spend years preparing for each new season by learning customs and consuming media.
- **Function**: Installed in Tutorial Guild Halls when a new dungeon opens. They walk stumbling crawlers through the Tutorial, HUD, and menus.
- **Capabilities**: They have access to the Dungeon Codex but NOT chat. They can share limited info (restricted by anti-cheat rules), review boss fight notes, and give tips after the fact.
- **Vulnerabilities**: They cannot be seen by live viewers (only press pass holders). They have NO death protections and can be permanently killed, which is why they stay in safe rooms.
- **Progression**: Crawlers lose access to their Game Guide upon descending to Floor 4 UNLESS they pick a class with the Manager Benefit.
- **Manager Benefit**: Turns the Guide into a permanent Manager. The Manager loses Codex access but gains chat access, the ability to teleport to the crawler in safe rooms, and death protections starting on Floor 4.
- **Notable Canon Guide**: Mordecai (Rat Hooligan, Level 50).

## Class System
- **Selection Process**: Crawlers select their primary class on Floor 3 inside their Tutorial Guild Hall alongside their Game Guide.
- **Options**: The System AI generates 30+ personalized options based on stats, race, gear, and club memberships. The AI highlights 3 recommended options based purely on skills (NOT ratings). In Carl's season, a record 40,000 different class combinations were seen.
- **Base Classes**: Bard, Magic User, Necromancer, Rogue, Warlock, Boring Ol' Fighter.
- **Specialized Subclasses**: Jack-of-all-Trades, Wind Mage, NecroBard, Compensated Anarchist, etc.
- **Earth Classes**: Inspired by Earth culture. Selecting one grants a Silver Earth Box containing an Earth Hobby Potion.
- **Floor 6 Specialization**: Crawlers enter a staging area with 10 minutes to choose a subclass (no chat allowed), which changes their class name. If a class cannot be specialized here, they choose an Endorsement instead (class name remains unchanged).
- **Further Specialization**: Available on Floors 9 and 12.

## The Deal System (10ers / Exit Deals)
- **Floor 10 Survival**: Surviving to Floor 10 grants Syndicate Citizenship, allowing the use of Syndicate lawyers to negotiate an Exit Deal.
- **The Trade**: Crawlers trade their lives for indentured servitude to the production company (Borant). One cycle is roughly 16-34 standard years.
- **Roles**: Game Guides (the fastest way to work off debt), Demigod Attendants, Event Hosts.
- **Terms**: High ratings and rare achievements during the crawl result in better deal terms.
- **Completion Rate**: Nobody has ever completed the dungeon. Typically, all crawlers are dead by Floor 13.

## Sponsors
- **Overview**: Organizations or individuals who pay to advertise on a crawler's channel. They send Benefactor Boxes (rare, high-tier loot) to crawlers.
- **Limits**: Crawlers can have a maximum of 3 sponsors, acquired at the start of Floor 4, Floor 5, and Floor 6.
- **Auctions**: A 45-hour auction occurs when each floor opens, with bids starting at 1 credit.
- **Benefactor Boxes**: A higher bid above the standard fee means better Benefactor Box discounts for the crawler. A lower bid means the crawler pays more for boxes. All crawlers get sponsors, but only popular ones receive Benefactor Boxes.
- **Communication**: Via the HUD's Sponsorship Tab, a crawler can message their sponsor, but the sponsor cannot reply directly.
- **Floor 7**: Sponsors are allowed to transfer patronage.

## Guilds
- **Introduction**: Introduced on Floor 6. Separate parties and individuals can team up to share upgrades.
- **Formation**: A Guild Charter costs 500,000+ gold from a Bopca Protector.
- **Level 1 Guild**: Can have up to 5 parties/individuals. At least one member per party must own a Personal Space.
- **Benefits**: Adds a Guild Common Area to all associated personal spaces.
- **Management**: The Guild Leader controls membership, can assign admin rights, charge membership fees, and create a treasury.
- **Upgrades**: Personal space environmental upgrades can be shared (for a licensing fee), and guild-wide XP sharing can be purchased.

## Community Game Guides (from Bopca CCS)
Example NPCs for use in campaigns:
- **Bobby**: Level 40 Naga Cleric, Guild Affiliation: Club Vanquisher. Steers crawlers toward Club Vanquisher; helps otherwise unless they get a Club Vanquisher tattoo.
- **Master Farengar**: Level 50 Half Elf Mage. Was world-hopping between planes, got caught by Borant, and chose 50 seasons of guide service over death. Currently 32 seasons in.
- **Solstice**: Level 50 Cat Girl PR Rep. Survived to Floor 11 using only PR/fan mechanics. First season as a guide.
- **Topher**: Level 50 Null Artificer. A 10er who took a deal for too many seasons. Suffers from PTSD and blitz stick addiction. Not reliable, but genuinely knowledgeable if sober.
- **Vorren Ka**: Level 50 Veyr (four-armed alien). Former navigation officer accidentally entered into the dungeon. Now serving as a Guide after surviving and taking a Floor 10 deal.
- **Zanzi**: Level 65 Lajabless Evoker. Has two personalities—a cheerful/ditzy human form and a cold/competent skull form. Each is unaware of the other.

## Homebrew Notes (Rob's Session)
- **System**: Rob's homebrew uses the DCC TTRPG system (Dungeon Crawler Carl TTRPG by Renegade Game Studios).
- **Campaign Start**: Players are new crawlers on Floor 1. Cold open: Everything with a roof above ground collapses mid-scene. Everyone indoors is gone.
- **Objective**: Find the stairwell to Floor 2.
- **Floor 1 Tutorial Guild**: The NPC Guide is an ex-crawler serving their guide contract. The guide walks players through HUD mechanics, class previews, and system rules.
- **Class Selection**: Happens on Floor 3 (canon). The Manager Benefit mechanic needs to be designed.
- **Characters**: Pre-gen crawlers available are DORIS, QUILL, and MILES (for 3 players). Add FLEX for 4 players.
- **Meta Knowledge**: Nobody has ever completed the dungeon. All dead by Floor 13 typically.
